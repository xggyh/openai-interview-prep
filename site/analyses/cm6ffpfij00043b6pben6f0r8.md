## 题目本质

**"What signals are you looking for that an engineer is ready for promotion?"** —— Manager 题，1 人报告。考你**能不能清晰阐述 promotion rubric** 和你的**评估哲学**。

跟 "Tell me a time you promoted" 是配套题：前者考实操，后者考你的**思维框架**。

## 面试官想听什么

1. 你有**结构化思考**（不是凭感觉）
2. 你区分 **"在当前 level 表现好"** vs **"已经在做下一 level 的工作"**
3. 你考虑 **scope + judgment + impact**，不只是技术
4. 你**避免常见 bias**（最近 promo 过的人、和自己像的人）

## 核心信号框架

升级判断的金科玉律：

> **"You promote when someone has been operating at the next level consistently for 6+ months, not when they're 'ready' to try."**

不是"潜力"，是"已经在做"。

## 三层信号

### 1. Scope（最 visible，最容易被滥用）

**问题**：scope 不是 "他做了大项目"，而是 **他独立 own 多大的 problem space**。

| Level | Scope 信号 |
|---|---|
| Mid → Senior | Own 一个 feature area，driving 3+ person efforts |
| Senior → Staff | Own 一个 system / cross-team initiative，影响 quarterly OKR |
| Staff → Principal | Set yearly tech strategy for 多 team org |

陷阱：被分配大项目 ≠ own 大 scope。要看 **他能不能在没人 hand-hold 下完成**。

### 2. Judgment（最 invisible，最有信号）

**关键问题**：他做 trade-off 决策时考虑得怎么样？

具体信号：
- **Pre-mortem**：他能不能在 design doc 里写出 risks + mitigation，而不是只写 happy path？
- **Disagreement quality**：当他不同意你 / PM 时，是基于 data 还是 emotion？
- **Push-back judgment**：他能不能拒绝"做某事"并 articulate why？反之过度服从是 red flag。
- **Cost/value tradeoff**：他报 estimate 时考虑 maintenance / opportunity cost 吗？

陷阱：技术 sharp 不等于 judgment 好。新晋 Senior 经常技术爆但 judgment 还是 Mid level —— 不该升 Staff。

### 3. Impact（最 quantifiable，但最 tricky）

**问题**：impact 不是 "他做了 X"，而是 **"如果他不做，X 还会发生吗？unique contribution"**。

| 类型 | 例子 |
|---|---|
| Direct impact | 他写的代码 ship 给用户、driving 业务指标 |
| Multiplier impact | 他建的工具 / library 加速了 N 人 |
| Cultural impact | 他建立的 code review 文化 / on-call 流程 |
| Recruiting / mentoring impact | 他带的 junior 升级、他帮招的人 |

Staff+ 应该有 **multiplier** 信号，不能只靠 individual impact。

## 反信号 (Red Flags) —— 看起来 ready 但其实不是

1. **Loud but shallow**：很多 broadcast 邮件 / 大会议发言，但深挖具体技术贡献都是别人做的
2. **Hero work**：靠 heroics 救火，但**不建立防火机制** → impact 不可持续
3. **过度依赖你**：每周 1:1 要你给方向，离开你独立无法决策
4. **Cherry-picking 项目**：只挑有 visibility 的，拒绝苦活
5. **Promo-driven**：每次对话都聚焦"我什么时候升"，不聚焦"做好这件事"

## "Operating at next level" 怎么判断

不是问"他做了什么"，而是 **"假设他升了，第二天他的 work pattern 需要变化多少？"**

- 几乎不变 → ready
- 中等变化 → not yet, 3-6 个月后
- 大变化 → not yet, 12+ 个月

## 我的回答模板（2-3 分钟）

> "I think about promotion in three dimensions: scope, judgment, and impact.
> 
> **Scope** is the most visible but easiest to misread. I look for whether the engineer is independently owning a problem space — not just doing big tasks I assigned, but choosing what's important and driving it. For Senior, that means one feature area; for Staff, a cross-team system.
> 
> **Judgment** is the hardest to see but most signal-rich. I watch how they handle disagreement, how they think about trade-offs, how they pre-mortem risks before they ship. A Senior can build the right thing; a Staff knows when *not* to build something.
> 
> **Impact** I qualify as multiplier vs direct. At Staff level, I expect to see other engineers' productivity going up because of what this person builds — tools, abstractions, or culture — not just their own ship velocity.
> 
> The single test I use: 'If they were promoted tomorrow, would their work-pattern need to change much?' If yes, they're not ready yet — they'd be growing into the role, not validating it.
> 
> The red flags I watch for: loud-but-shallow signals, hero work without systemic fix, over-dependence on me for direction, cherry-picking only visible projects, and promo-driven conversations. None of these alone disqualifies, but combined they tell me to slow down.
> 
> Last piece: I separate 'ready' from 'evidence I can defend in calibration'. Even if I personally believe they're ready, if I can't bring 2-3 concrete artifacts (design doc they led, cross-team migration they drove, junior they mentored to promo), the system can't validate it. So I optimize for both the substance and the artifacts."

## 不同 level 的具体差异

### Mid → Senior
- 从"被分配任务"到"自己定义 scope"
- 从"写好代码"到"做出 good trade-off"
- 从"problem solver"到"problem framer"

### Senior → Staff
- 从"team 内 expert"到"cross-team 影响"
- 从"做技术决策"到"shape technical direction"
- 从"用 tool"到"build / choose tools for org"

### Staff → Principal
- 从"项目级影响"到"年度 / 多年级影响"
- 从"被人 reference"到"set the standard"
- 从"技术 thought leader"到"organizational thought leader"

## OpenAI 加分项

- 提到**evidence-based calibration**：你不只看感觉，要能给出 artifacts
- 提到 **DEI lens**：你 actively 检查 bias —— promo rate 是否在 underrepresented groups 公平
- 提到 **calibration politics**：你提前为下属做 visibility / sponsorship 工作
- 提到 **AI safety / care**：在 OpenAI 这种 stakes 高的地方，judgment 信号特别重要 —— 不只是 ship 速度

## 易错点

> [!pitfall]
> ❌ 只说 "scope and impact" → 缺 judgment，听起来肤浅；
> ❌ 全在讲 IC 维度，不提 multiplier / org-level 信号 → Senior 答案，不像 Staff+；
> ❌ 没提 red flags / 反信号 → 显得没经验；
> ❌ "I just feel they're ready" → no framework；
> ❌ 不区分 "ready" vs "defensible in calibration" → 没经历过实战；
> ❌ 没提 promo politics → 太天真。

> [!key]
> Promo 决策的核心 mental model：**"已经在做下一 level"，不是"潜力"**。证据导向 + 多维评估 + 反 bias = strong Manager signal。

> [!followup]
> 可能追问：
> - "Have you ever promoted someone too early? What happened?"
> - "How do you give feedback to someone who thinks they're ready but isn't?"
> - "What about peer feedback — how do you weight it?"
> - "Have you held someone back? Walk through the conversation."
