Tell me a time you helped an engineer promote

People Management Manager 原题 ↗
OpenAI · Manager · 📋 1 · 🕒 Early January, 2025

Problem Statement

我的思路与解法

题目本质

"Tell me a time you helped an engineer get promoted" —— Manager / EM 题，1 人报告。考察你是否主动培养下属，而不是只关心 deliverables。

面试官想听什么

  1. 你不只是 task assigner，你关心下属的成长路径
  2. 你能识别当前 gap并给出具体的发展计划
  3. 你能create opportunities而不是等下属自己 figure it out
  4. 你能advocate为下属在 calibration 时争取

这一题是 Manager 必杀 题 —— 没准备过的人讲不出具体例子。

回答框架（CARL，3 分钟）

Context（30 秒）

  • 谁：直接下属，当时什么 level
  • 什么 gap：他想升到下一 level，差什么 signal
  • 时间窗口：6-12 个月

"I had a Mid-level engineer, Alex, who joined my team 18 months ago. Strong individual contributor — could ship features end-to-end and was technically solid. But he was missing two signals for Senior promotion: scope of impact (always picked self-contained projects) and tech leadership (rarely drove design discussions)."

Action（90 秒）—— 最重要的部分

这里要讲3-5 个 specific 干预，不是泛泛 "I gave him guidance"。

"I did four things over 9 months:

1) Co-defined his promo path explicitly. In our 1:1 I shared the Senior rubric line-by-line and said which signals he had and which he didn't. We picked the 2 missing ones to focus on.

2) Reassigned scope. I asked him to take ownership of our caching layer — a cross-team component used by 6 other teams. This forced him into design reviews and stakeholder management he previously avoided.

3) Set him up to fail forward. I asked him to lead the design doc for the next major refactor. I shadowed his first design review without speaking — let him own the room. After, we did a debrief on what worked / what didn't. We did 4 of these over the year.

4) Created visibility. I made sure his work showed up in the right places — invited him to org-level architecture review, asked him to present quarterly wrap to the VP. I also stopped CC'ing myself on his cross-team emails so他成为单点联络。

5) Worked with calibration politics. Two weeks before calibration, I pre-aligned with our director and two skip-level peers, showed them his design docs and the latency improvement (32%) and the cross-team adoption (4 of 6 teams)."

Result（30 秒）

"Promoted to Senior at the next cycle. More importantly, 6 months post-promo he was leading a 3-person sub-team and one of our directors asked specifically to have him on her org's roadmap review."

Learning / Reflection（30 秒）

"Two things I learned. First, promo is a leading indicator of long-term value, not a reward —— if I just gave him tasks and waited, he might've never gotten that signal organically. Second, calibration politics matter —— even with strong evidence, you have to do the legwork to make sure your director and skip-levels know the story before the room."

准备多个版本（不同失败模式）

版本 A：成功 promo（前面那个）

版本 B：尝试 promo 但没成

"I tried to promote my Senior engineer to Staff. We worked together for 14 months, set up a sub-team for him to lead, ran a successful migration. But at calibration he was held back — feedback was his 'org-wide impact' wasn't visible enough beyond our team.

What I learned: I didn't bring him into enough cross-org venues. I should have pushed him to present at engineering all-hands, write a tech blog, etc. We rebuilt the plan and he made it next cycle."

讲失败 + 学习 的版本反而更有信号 —— 说明你 reflect 过。

版本 C：复杂关系（下属表现下滑）

如果对方提的 prompt 是 "tell me a time you helped someone struggling"，准备一个 "I unblocked them from drowning" 故事 —— 不是 promo，是 retention。

OpenAI 加分项

  • 提到stretch project：让 IC 接超出当前 level 的事情（控失败成本）
  • Sponsorship 而非 mentorship：花你自己的 political capital 给下属创造机会
  • Calibration craft：你懂 cross-promo 的政治游戏，不是天真等评估
  • 提到帮其他组的人 —— 不只是直系 reports

易错点

易错点 / Red Flag

❌ "I told them to work harder" → 没具体动作； ❌ "I just gave them feedback" → 太被动； ❌ 故事的下属是个 senior 帮 junior → 不是你直接做的事； ❌ 没数字（impact / scope / timeline）→ 无法验证； ❌ 全是 "I I I" 没提下属自己 agency → 听起来像你 over-managed； ❌ 跳过 calibration politics 这部分 → 面试官知道你没真做过 promo。

核心要点

Manager 能力评估的核心：你能不能在 6 个月时间内培养出一个比 6 个月前实质更强的下属，且能说服系统给这个下属升职。讲故事时要展示 意识 + 计划 + 干预 + 倡导 四阶段。

延伸 / Follow-up

可能追问：

  • "What if the person wasn't ready?" → 你能不能诚实告诉他 not yet + 设新目标？
  • "What if your director disagreed?" → 你的 calibration 准备能不能反驳？
  • "Tell me a time you DIDN'T promote someone who wanted it" → 准备一个 hard conversation 故事
  • "How do you balance promo focus vs current delivery?" → 你怎么不牺牲短期产出
